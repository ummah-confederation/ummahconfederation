POLICY II : REST & GHAFLAH MANAGEMENT

PREAMBLE

This policy establishes principles for proper rest and the avoidance of heedlessness (ghaflah) after demanding work. It recognizes that human beings require cooldown phases to release cognitive and physical tension, while acknowledging that such moments are opportunities for shaytan to lead us astray into useless or sinful activity. The policy distinguishes between beneficial rest (worship and social bonding) and harmful ghaflah (mindless consumption), providing clear guidance for government members.

TITLE 1 : PRINCIPLES OF BENEFICIAL REST

Article 1 – The Nature of Rest

Each significant job, especially worldly work, is cognitively and physically demanding. Rest is necessary and permissible. However, the quality and nature of rest determines whether it leads to benefit or harm. There is a saying that whoever rests from demanding work and their rest is not upon a sunnah, then they rest upon disobedience (maksiah). While the exact chain of this narration requires verification, its wisdom is sound: rest should be purposeful and aligned with beneficial activities.

Article 2 – Categories of Permissible Rest

Permissible forms of rest for government members fall into two categories:

  1. Ibadah-Based Rest: Worship activities that relax the mind and body, including: reciting Quran (while sitting facing the qiblah, not lying down), dhikr and supplication, voluntary prayers, and seeking Islamic knowledge (or truth-seeking in general).
  2. Social Rest: Networking and bonding with people, including: conversations with family, friends, or colleagues, community gatherings and social activities, and building and maintaining relationships.

Both forms serve legitimate purposes. Ibadah provides spiritual renewal and reward. Social interaction strengthens bonds that may serve beneficial purposes later, biidhnillah, and is not inherently procrastination but rather progress in building valuable relationships.

Article 3 – The Quran as Cooldown

When experiencing cognitive tension or brain fog after demanding work, the primary recommended sunnah activity is reciting Quran while sitting and facing the qiblah. Shaytan often whispers that you are unable to release tension through Quran recitation, leading you toward haram activities such as lying down and scrolling. Resist this whisper. If brain fog persists after Quran recitation, seek Allah's help through more supplication, or shift to other beneficial activities: either social interaction (non-solitary) or physical sunnah activities even if it is solitary (because inshaallah it is an ibadah too). Struggle against the temptation to default to mindless consumption. Bismillah.

TITLE 2 : FORBIDDEN GHAFLAH

Article 4 – Scrolling Without Keyword (SWK)

Scrolling without a keyword means you do not have a particular direction or objective to achieve. It usually aims to "relax" your brain after a hard task to relieve mental tension, but this is actually harmful. As a government, you need to network with people as a form of entertainment, not engage in solitary entertainment or activity (ibadah is allowed though). Note that SWK is different from scrolling with a keyword. When you have a keyword to search, it means you are in a research session to gather data that will later be analyzed and interpreted into a useful output (such as policies, papers, and so on). By engaging in SWK, it indicates that you are unaware that you still have many objectives or projects that have not been completed. Even networking purely for entertainment is more useful. Bonding with humans is not inherently procrastination; rather, it is progress in building relationships that may serve another purpose later biidhnillah.

Article 5 – Video Watching

As a government, it is unethical to watch edited videos, especially without a keyword. You must preserve your brain energy as much as possible. Edited videos—particularly those with frequent cuts, music, visual effects, and sound effects—are cognitively draining. That is why you have assistants: to translate any important content, regardless of format, into written form that is relevant for a government leader to notice. Not every piece of content deserves your attention as a government leader.

Article 6 – News SWK

Most news algorithms function as echo chambers with which you have no real connection, especially if you are a layperson without a direct role in global affairs. What is the use of reading news from another continent if you have no influence there or if you are not researching something? News should primarily come from your own observation or be received through your intelligence network. Its purpose is to inform you of matters on which you can take direct action.

Article 7 – Solitary Digital Entertainment

All forms of solitary digital entertainment are forbidden as rest activities. This includes but is not limited to: social media browsing without purpose, watching entertainment videos, playing games alone, and consuming digital content in isolation.

The distinction is clear: solitary ibadah (worship) is encouraged; solitary entertainment is forbidden. If you seek entertainment or relaxation beyond ibadah, engage in social activities that build relationships and bonds with people.

TITLE 3 : IMPLEMENTATION GUIDANCE

Article 8 – Decision Framework for Rest

When experiencing the need for rest after demanding work, follow this decision framework:

  1. Step 1: Attempt ibadah-based rest (preferably Quran recitation while sitting facing qiblah).
  2. Step 2: If tension persists, increase supplication and ask Allah for relief.
  3. Step 3: If still unable to focus on ibadah, shift to social rest (networking, conversation, community activity).
  4. Step 4: Consider physical sunnah activities (walking, light exercise in permissible ways, as it falls under solitary ibadah inshaallah).
  5. Never default to: Lying down and scrolling, watching videos without purpose, or any form of solitary digital entertainment.

Article 9 – Exceptions and Clarifications

Purpose-driven digital activity is not forbidden, even if conducted alone. This includes: research with specific keywords, work-related content review, educational materials with clear objectives, and communication necessary for duties.

The key distinction is intentionality and purpose. If an activity has a clear objective that advances your responsibilities, knowledge, social bond or ibadah, it is permissible. If it is merely to "pass time" or "relax mindlessly," it falls under forbidden ghaflah.
