GUIDELINE II : SSC DESIGN GUIDELINE

PREAMBLE

This document defines how media operations are structured within Ummah Media Worldwide. SSC is Segment -> Series -> Content.

TITLE 1 : MEDIA HIERARCHY

Article 1 – Institution (Entity)

Ummah Media Worldwide is the parent institution regarding media in Daarussalaam facing directly to the audience. It provides vision, governance, and long-term direction for all media operations.

Article 2 – Segment (Role)

A segment represents a bounded social role performed in public. Each segment has a clear function, scope, and responsibility. Because media is human-facing, each segment is understood as a role rather than a mechanical process.

Article 3 – Series (Program)

A series (also referred to as a program or show) is a branded, recurring content line operating within a segment. Multiple series may exist under the same segment, each with its own title, tone, and aesthetic.

Article 4 – Content (Asset)

Content refers to individual media outputs (posts, reels, carousels, etc.). Each content item belongs to a specific series and is distributed through an official account.

TITLE 2 : CORE DESIGN PRINCIPLES

Article 1 – Operational Clarity

Each segment must clearly define:

  • Inputs (what information comes in)
  • Process (how it is handled)
  • Outputs (what is published)

Article 2 – Legibility to the Audience

A segment and its series should be immediately understandable without explanation. The audience should intuitively recognize the role being performed and its boundaries.

Article 3 – Design by Current Capacity

Segments and series are created according to current capacity (time, budget, skills, and network). Roles that cannot yet be performed properly must be deferred. Production ambition must match operational reality.

TITLE 3 : FIRST SEGMENT — INDEXER

Article 1 – Segment Definition

The first segment operated by Ummah Media Worldwide is the Indexer. The Indexer’s role is to systematically collect, organize, and surface information of value to the community.

The Indexer functions as a utility segment. Its purpose is not hype or personality-driven engagement, but long-term usefulness. The audience follows the account because it is practically valuable and costly to miss.

Article 2 – Inputs

Typical inputs include Qur’anic verses, Hadith, scholarly quotations, public discussions, and other indexable materials relevant to the community.

Article 3 – Series Within the Indexer Segment

Multiple series may operate under the Indexer segment. Each series has its own name, branding, and tone, while remaining fully aligned with the Indexer role.

The existence of multiple series does not imply multiple segments. All series inherit the boundaries and responsibilities of the Indexer segment.

Article 4 – Process

Inputs are logged as they appear and transformed into content according to the relevant series guidelines, including visual style, tone, and framing.

Article 5 – Outputs

Outputs consist of published content assets (e.g., Instagram posts, reels, or carousels). Each asset is attributed to a specific series and distributed through official Ummah Media channels.

TITLE 4 : FUTURE SEGMENTS

Article 1 – Expansion, Independence & Document Updates

As institutional capacity increases, and by the permission of God, additional segments may be established. Each new segment represents a distinct social role and a clearly bounded function.

Every segment remains operationally independent and focused exclusively on its designated role. The creation of any new segment requires the addition of a corresponding title within this document to formally record and govern its existence.

Article 2 – Explainer Segment

An Explainer segment may be introduced when sufficient production capacity exists. Its role is to explain concepts clearly, often through long-form or video-based media.

Article 3 – Diaspora Connector Segment

A Connector segment may be introduced once sufficient network access and verification capacity exist. Its role is to surface opportunities, initiatives, and connections to the audience.
