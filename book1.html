BOOK I : THE CONSTITUTION

PREAMBLE

This Constitution establishes the foundational principles upon which all legislative, executive and judicative acts within the Abode of Peace (Daarussalaam) are built, ordered by the verses of Surah Al-Fatihah.

TITLE 1 : SURAH 1 VERSE 1

Article 1 – Translation

"In the name of Allah, The Most Compassionate, The Most Merciful"

Article 2 – Mandate of Divine Instrumentality

All legislative, executive, and judicial acts shall be done in the name of Allah, the Most Compassionate, The Most Merciful. Those exercising such authority are not the source of power, but instruments through which God's compassion and mercy flow into the world:

  1. Any legislative, executive, or judicial act devoid of compassion and mercy violates the principle of divine instrumentality.
  2. Any legislative, executive, or judicial act filled with compassion and mercy is an act of God alone. Human authorities claim neither authorship nor credit, serving solely as instruments.
    1. When you do good for yourself in the name of Allah, God is channeling His compassion and mercy to you through your own hands. If you believe you are the doer, it feels empty—because you're only acting for yourself. But when you realize He has done this for you through you, your heart fills with the awareness of divine presence and care in every act and taqdeer.
    2. When one does good for others in the name of Allah, mercy reaches them through you, the servant. Believing “I did this for them” breeds entitlement. Realizing “Allah relieved them, and I was allowed to witness it” dissolves the self into humility and surrender in every act and taqdeer.

(NB) To test whether an act fulfills this mandate of divine instrumentality, ask yourself: Can I invoke the name of Allah while performing this action? Can I publicly declare that this was done in His name? Acts lacking compassion and mercy typically demand secrecy—they cannot withstand the light of public declaration under His name. However, public declaration alone does not prove compliance, as wrongdoing can be brazenly proclaimed under divine invocation. Likewise, acts committed publicly without invoking His name are not automatically non-compliant, as wrongdoing can occur without divine attribution. Therefore, every act must be evaluated on its substance under subsection (1).

TITLE 2 : SURAH 1 VERSE 2-4

Article 1 – Translation

"All praise is for Allah—Rabb of all worlds."
"The Most Compassionate, Most Merciful."
"Malik of the Day of Judgment."

Article 2 – Hamd

The most devastating thought a human being can have in their low is to think they don't belong there—that they're meant to be somewhere else. This can lead to deep depression, even to killing themselves over it. The most detrimental thought a human being can have in their high is to think they do belong there—that this is where they're meant to be. This can lead to deep hubris, even to killing others over it. Hamd neutralizes all positions. The low are alleviated, the high humbled. The low are alleviated by realizing their position is not a mistake—it's the placement of the Most Merciful. The high are humbled by realizing their position becomes a mistake the moment compassion is gone. Through praising God (hamd), all stand equally, shoulder to shoulder, serving as instruments of the Divine.

Article 3 – Rabb

The low are alleviated by understanding who placed them there: the Rabb—the One who originates all things and who, through continual sustenance and nurture, brings them toward their proper perfection. Your position is not an error to escape, but a placement by the One who is actively bringing you toward completion.

Article 4 – Malik

The high are humbled by understanding who granted them their position: the Malik—the One who, by right of origination, holds dominion over all things; and by responsibility for sustenance, renders judgment upon those who corrupt what was ordained for perfection—not from a disposition toward judgment, but because justice, when violated, demands restoration at the claim of the exploited party. Your elevation is not ownership to defend by any means, but a trust held under the gaze of the One who will hold you accountable for how you wielded it.

Article 5 – Mandate of Excellence

Because we stand between Rabb and Malik—sustained by one, accountable to the other—we are bound to the Mandate of Excellence:

  1. Moral Accountability: All persons shall act as if they see their Rabb; and if they do not see Him, then indeed their Rabb sees them.
  2. Legal Accountability: All persons shall act as if they see their Malik; and if they do not see Him, then indeed their Malik sees them.

(NB) The Core Question on Excellence: Can you make every movement—small or large, alone or in company—an act of worship / gratitude to Rabb & Malik?

TITLE 3 : SURAH 1 VERSE 5

Article 1 – Translation

"You ˹alone˺ we worship and You ˹alone˺ we ask for help."

Article 2 – Mandate of The Great Independence

Having established who deserves praise (Rabb and Malik) and how to conduct ourselves under their gaze (excellence), we now declare where ultimate worship and reliance must be directed:

  1. Ibadah to True God (Rabb & Malik): To Him alone we direct our ultimate worship and obedience. Not self or other entities.
  2. Isti'anah to True God (Rabb & Malik): To Him alone we direct our ultimate trust and reliance. Not self or other entities.

(NB) Principle of Loyalty Revelation: That which a person continues to worship and obey when worship and obedience entail genuine cost constitutes their true God. This loyalty may be maintained progressively as resources decline, but the ultimate test manifests when all resources are exhausted. At that juncture, whatever one resorts to and relies upon—if doing so requires the willful abandonment of one’s professed object of worship and obedience, or the peaceful acceptance of such abandonment—reveals one’s true God.

TITLE 4 : SURAH 1 VERSE 6

Article 1 – Translation

"Guide us along the Straight Path"

Article 2 – Huda

Huda is (1) the divine direction toward recognizing the truth and (2) the divine assistance that enables action upon it. Toward this we turn to God seventeen times each day in humility and reliance, pleading for it so that God, through huda, may enable us to preserve the mandate of the great independence: directing exclusive Ibadah and Istiʿanah to the True God.

Article 3 – Recognized Truth

  1. A Posteriori Truth.
  2. A Priori Truth.
  3. Islamic Truth.

Article 4 – Mandate of Constant Huda Seeking

Principle of Truth as a Basis of Action:

  1. Assumption: An assumption, when used as a basis for action, can sometimes be useful, because people or even animal, at different levels of experience rely on different degrees of intuition or instinct. However, for people with limited experience, acting primarily on assumptions often leads to reactionary decisions. These decisions may become too costly to win (a Pyrrhic victory), too cheap to lose (premature defeat), or result in no decision at all, as conflicting assumptions block one another and cause paralysis (buntu).
  2. Half Truth: A half truth contains elements of correctness but lacks critical context or completeness. When used as a basis for action, half truth is dangerous because it creates a false sense of certainty while hiding key risks or consequences. Acting on half truth often leads to misjudgment, escalation of conflict, or irreversible mistakes, and therefore should be avoided as a foundation for decision-making.
  3. Near Truth: Near truth represents information that is not fully verified but is sufficiently accurate and context-aware to support timely action. Near truth is acceptable when time constraints, uncertainty, or rapidly evolving situations make full verification impractical. In such cases, delaying action in pursuit of complete truth may lead to greater chaos, loss, or harm than acting on a near truth.
  4. True Truth: True truth is a state of comprehensive, verified understanding, where facts, context, and consequences are clearly known. It is the ideal basis for action (avoiding pyrrhic victory and premature defeat biidhnillah) and should be pursued whenever time and conditions allow. However, the pursuit of true truth must not paralyze decision-making when action is urgently required.

TITLE 5 : SURAH 1 VERSE 7

Article 1 – Translation

"The Path of those You have blessed—not those You are displeased with, or those who are astray"

Article 2 – Mandate of Restorative Endeavor

Principle of Alignment Revelation: Trials reveal which party a person truly belongs to—not by creating their character, but by exposing it under pressure. When the cost of pursuing truth becomes real and resources begin to fail, the endeavors one continues to produce disclose true alignment. This revelation may unfold gradually as hardship intensifies, but becomes unmistakable when the choice between restorative and destructive action can no longer be avoided.

  1. The Blessed Party: Those who know the truth and act upon it, producing restorative endeavors (Biidhnillah) to achieve the Great Independence.
  2. The Maghdub Party: Those who know the truth but refuse to act upon it, thereby producing oppression—knowingly or unknowingly—through destructive endeavors.
  3. The Dhallin Party: Those who mistake falsehood for truth and act upon it, thereby unknowingly sustaining oppression through destructive endeavors initiated by the Maghdub Party.
  4. The Burdened Party: Those who neither initiate nor sustain destructive endeavors, yet are compelled, without consent, to bear their oppressive consequences.

Article 3 – Consider this Example

In peaceful, prosperous times, nearly all who hold authority profess to serve justice, the people, and compassion. Politicians, bureaucrats, and judges alike proclaim noble intentions. During such stability, it becomes difficult to distinguish who genuinely produces restorative endeavors from those who merely perform them.

When trials arrive—economic collapse, war, scandal, uprising—the alignment of each party becomes manifest:

  1. The Blessed Party maintains principle even at personal cost. They refuse bribes when broke, protect the vulnerable when protection becomes dangerous, and speak truth when lies would preserve their position.
  2. The Maghdub Party is exposed. They knew the policy would harm the poor. They knew corruption was occurring. Yet they chose power and wealth over truth, abandoning what they recognized as right.
  3. The Dhallin Party is revealed. Well-meaning officials who genuinely believed the propaganda, who implemented harmful policies thinking they served the good, who sustained oppression without realizing they were instruments of destruction.
  4. The Burdened Party becomes visible. Ordinary people who initiated nothing, sustained nothing, yet bear the full weight of decisions made above them without their consent.

This pattern repeats across history: the fall of regimes, the rise of tyrannies, economic catastrophes, and revolutions all function as trials that strip away pretense and reveal true character. Those in authority fear such crises not merely for the instability they bring, but because trials expose what prosperity conceals—who truly serves and who merely claims to serve.

TITLE 6 : SUMMARY

Article 1 – The 5 Mandates

  4. When Deciding: Mandate of Constant Huda Seeking.
  5. After Deciding: Mandate of Restorative Endeavor.
