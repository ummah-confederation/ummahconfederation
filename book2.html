TITLE 2 : TEXTUAL LEGENDS

Article 1 – Square Brackets [...]

  1. Definition: Square brackets are used to declare or specify the type, category, or classification of an entity.
  2. Example: “General Public [Community]”.

Article 2 – Parentheses (...)

  1. Definition: Parentheses are used to clarify, qualify, or provide additional information within a unit of text.
  2. Example: “Hoofed animals (such as horses)”.

Article 3 – Curly Brackets {...}

  1. Definition: Curly brackets are used to cite a source, including but not limited to body notes or references.
  2. Example: “... My eyes sleep, but my heart does not sleep.” {Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī}.
